Czech instruments: Modernisation Fund, OP TAK, RES+

EU money does not arrive from Brussels with your name on it. It is handed to national operators who package it into local programmes with Czech names and Czech rules. To use funding in Czechia you have to know who runs which pot — and which pot fits your project.

Learning objective

By the end you can name the Czech operators that actually run EU energy funding — SFŽP ČR, MPO with OP TAK via API, the Národní plán obnovy and NRB — and route a solar, BESS or efficiency project to the right one.

The delivery layer, explained simply

In the last lesson we drew the EU-level map. But a factory owner in Brno does not apply to "the Modernisation Fund" in the abstract. They apply to a Czech national programme that the EU fund pays for, run by a Czech operator, under a specific call.

Think of it as two layers. The EU layer holds the money and sets the broad rules. The delivery layer — the Czech agencies — translates that into concrete programmes with application forms, deadlines and eligibility tests. Your job as an originator is to know the delivery layer cold, because that is where the application actually lands.

Who runs what in Czechia

These are the real institutions. Learn them as named operators, each with its own turf.

  • SFŽP ČR (Státní fond životního prostředí ČR — the State Environmental Fund) operates the Modernisation Fund programmes in Czechia. That includes RES+ for new solar and other renewables, plus storage and efficiency programmes. This is the first stop for most Czech renewable-energy grants.
  • RES+ — the SFŽP programme under the Modernisation Fund aimed specifically at new renewable generation, including solar. The natural home for a ground-mount or larger rooftop solar project.
  • Nová zelená úsporám (New Green Savings) — another SFŽP programme, focused mainly on buildings: rooftop PV and storage tied to a building, plus efficiency measures.
  • MPO (Ministerstvo průmyslu a obchodu — the Ministry of Industry & Trade) runs OP TAK, the operational programme "Technologie a aplikace pro konkurenceschopnost" (2021–27, ERDF-funded), with energy-saving and renewables-for-business calls.
  • API (Agentura pro podnikání a inovace) — the agency that actually administers OP TAK calls for companies. When a firm applies under OP TAK, it deals with API.
  • Národní plán obnovy — the Czech National Recovery Plan, the route through which RRF recovery money (with green-energy components) flows.
  • NRB (Národní rozvojová banka — the National Development Bank) — the Czech promotional bank, which co-finances and provides guarantees alongside grants.
  • CzechInvest — the investment and business-development agency that helps investors navigate Czech incentives.
Key term · SFŽP ČR

The Czech State Environmental Fund — the national operator of the Modernisation Fund in Czechia. It runs the programmes that matter most to renewables, including RES+ for new solar and renewables and Nová zelená úsporám for buildings. For a Czech solar or storage grant, this is where the application ecosystem begins.

Key term · OP TAK (administered by API)

OP TAK (Operační program Technologie a aplikace pro konkurenceschopnost) is the EU-co-funded business-competitiveness programme run by MPO and administered by the agency API. It carries energy-saving and renewables-for-business calls — the route a company often uses for efficiency and self-consumption projects.

Which instrument fits which project

The mistake newcomers make is treating "EU funding" as one door. It is several doors, and the project type chooses the door.

A new solar plant — ground-mounted or a substantial rooftop generating to sell or self-consume — points first to RES+ under the Modernisation Fund at SFŽP ČR. A battery storage project also looks to SFŽP storage programmes under the Modernisation Fund, sometimes paired with the generation it supports. An energy-efficiency project at a business — better motors, heat recovery, process upgrades, and often self-consumption PV — frequently fits OP TAK through MPO/API. Buildings-led rooftop and storage can route through Nová zelená úsporám.

The exact programme, the open call and the eligible costs change over time, so confirm the live call before you commit a client to a route. The mapping below is the durable logic; the specifics are always "check the current call".

Across the region

The Czech Modernisation Fund / RES+ has DACH siblings — same product, different rails. Germany supports renewables through the EEG (Marktprämie + competitive auctions), with KfW providing the cheap finance. Austria uses the EAG (market premium + investment grants). Switzerland, outside the EU, gives PV a one-time investment grant — the Einmalvergütung — rather than feed-in or premium.

In the room

How to show a Czech client you know the local landscape, not just the Brussels headlines:

Sales script

For your solar, the route would be RES+ under the Modernisation Fund, run by the State Environmental Fund. If we add storage, that sits under the same Modernisation Fund storage programmes. And the efficiency upgrades on your production line may qualify under OP TAK through the agency API. We would line these up against the open calls and the development bank for co-financing — but the first move is to confirm which calls are live right now.

Objections you will hear

Do I apply directly to the EU for this?
Rebuttal

For most Czech solar and storage projects, no — you apply through the Czech operator. The Modernisation Fund is run nationally by SFŽP ČR, and OP TAK business calls are administered by API. The EU provides the money and the framework; the Czech agency runs the call you actually enter.

My project is solar plus storage plus an efficiency upgrade — does one programme cover it all?
Rebuttal

Usually not one programme — different elements fit different doors. New solar tends to fit RES+, storage fits the Modernisation Fund storage programmes, and the efficiency works can fit OP TAK. We structure the project so each part is matched to its right instrument, then check that the calls are open and the costs are eligible.

How do I know which call is open and how much it pays?
Rebuttal

We verify it live. Call windows, budgets and eligible costs change, so any figure I gave you from memory could be stale. The reliable answer comes from the operator's own site — sfzp.cz for SFŽP programmes, the API site for OP TAK — at the moment we apply. That is exactly why "check the current call" is the rule.
