DACH — Germany & Austria

Two deep EU markets: Germany's EEG auctions and mature PPA scene, Austria's hydro base and EAG. Same EU frame, different national support scheme.

DACH — Switzerland

In the region, not in the EU. Its own regulator (ElCom), grid (Swissgrid) and PV grant (Einmalvergütung). EU market integration is signed but not in force.

How EU law reaches your project

The frame is set in Brussels;
the rules are run at home.

1

The EU sets the target

A Directive (e.g. RED III's 42.5% renewables by 2030) sets the goal; a Regulation (e.g. the Electricity Market Regulation) applies directly.

2

Bodies coordinate it

The Commission (DG ENER) drives policy; ACER coordinates national regulators; ENTSO-E runs grid codes and the coupled day-ahead market.

3

Each country transposes it

Member states write the Directive into national law on their own timeline — becoming the EEG (DE), EAG (AT), or Modernisation-Fund programmes (CZ).

4

A regulator + TSO run the market

BNetzA/ČEPS, E-Control/APG, ElCom/Swissgrid — the bodies your project actually deals with day to day.

The drivers that don't change

Lead with these — they're the same
in Prague, Berlin, Vienna and Zurich.

RED III

Binding 42.5% renewables by 2030 (45% indicative). Plus renewables-acceleration areas for faster permitting.

2040 target — now law

A binding 90% net cut in greenhouse gases vs 1990 by 2040, written into the European Climate Law (in force ~April 2026).

Market-design reform

The 2024 reform actively promotes PPAs and two-way CfDs; two-way CfDs become mandatory for new price-support schemes from July 2027.

EU ETS + CBAM

A carbon price on industry, plus a border carbon adjustment from 2026 — making clean power a competitiveness issue, not just compliance.

ETS2 (2028)

A second carbon market for buildings and transport, launching 2028 — it eventually absorbs national prices like Germany's nEHS.

CEEAG + GBER

The EU state-aid rules that cap how much subsidy any project may take — the frame every national grant scheme fits inside (CH excepted).

In the room — same product, different rails

The reason this works isn't local — it's European. The EU has fixed the target, put a price on carbon, and rewritten the market to favour long-term clean-power contracts. That's true whether you're in Brno, Munich or Graz. What changes is who you file with and which support scheme you tap. So let me show you the number on your site, then the local route to get there.
