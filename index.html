Why this is a business

Three forces turned European energy into one of the
largest commercial opportunities of the decade.

Price volatility

European power prices are set at the margin by gas. The 2022 shock pushed industrial tariffs to record highs and never fully reverted. A fixed-price PPA replaces that uncertainty with a number a CFO can plan around.

Decarbonisation is mandatory

RED III, CBAM and corporate net-zero targets turn clean power from a preference into a requirement. Scope 2 emissions are now a board-level liability — and renewable PPAs are the cleanest way to cut them.

Solar is already cheaper

The levelised cost of new solar and storage now sits below the retail and industrial grid tariff across most of Europe. You are not selling a sacrifice — you are selling a lower bill that also happens to be green.

The course is taught through four lenses. Czechia runs through it as the worked example — OTE spot pricing, the ERÚ tariff stack, the Modernisation Fund — while the DACH region (Germany, Austria, Switzerland) and the EU-government layer (the Commission, RED III, the 2024 market-design reform pushing PPAs and CfDs) show how the same logic plays out across Europe. The principle is identical everywhere; the regulator and the support scheme change.
