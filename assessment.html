Are you sales-ready?

Fifteen questions across the whole academy — market mechanics, the financial models, PPAs, the exit, EU funding and the country markets. Answer all, then score it. 11/15 or better = sales-ready.

1.What usually sets the wholesale electricity price in a European market?

Marginal pricing: the last unit dispatched sets the price, so gas often sets it even with lots of cheap renewables.

2.The core thing a PPA gives a customer is:

A PPA replaces volatile spot exposure with a contracted price a CFO can plan around.

3.LCOE is:

Levelised Cost of Energy — capex + lifetime opex over lifetime generation. The number you beat the grid with.

4.Apply a lower discount rate to the same future cash flows. The value:

Lower required yield → higher NPV. That re-pricing is yield compression — the engine of the exit.

5.In a develop-to-sell model, the big gain mostly comes from:

You take development risk at a high hurdle; a fund buys the safe cash at a low yield → the spread is the exit.

6.Which country is NOT in the EU internal electricity market today?

Switzerland trades bilaterally; the EU-CH Bilaterals III electricity agreement is signed (Mar 2026) but not in force.

7.Germany's main renewables support law is:

EEG = Germany (market premium + auctions). EAG = Austria. Stromgesetz = Switzerland.

8.The EU's largest corporate-PPA market by volume is:

Spain leads on volume (Italy ~#2), thanks to the EU's cheapest solar — though capture-price cannibalisation is the catch.

9.Which market has the EU's highest carbon intensity and is the biggest Modernisation Fund beneficiary?

Poland's coal grid means high carbon-driven prices — a strong PPA case — and it draws the largest Modernisation Fund share.

10.The Modernisation Fund is available to:

It's an ETS-financed fund for 13 lower-income members; wealthier states use RRF/PERTE/PNRR instead.

11.Under the EU market-design reform, two-way CfDs become mandatory for new direct price-support schemes from:

The reform (in force 16 Jul 2024) makes two-way CfDs mandatory for new price support concluded on/after 17 Jul 2027.

12.To receive an EU grant or Modernisation Fund money, the beneficiary must be:

A non-EU parent can own the platform but can't be the grant beneficiary; the local SPV is. (Educational, not advice.)

13.BESS "revenue stacking" means:

A grid battery layers energy arbitrage, capacity payments and ancillary (e.g. FCR) into one revenue.

14.In Italy, the development risk the deep-dive flags most is:

High prices make the case strong, but regional authorisation/aree-idonee is the #1 bottleneck.

15.DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) matters most to:

DSCR tells a lender the project comfortably covers its debt payments — central to debt sizing.
