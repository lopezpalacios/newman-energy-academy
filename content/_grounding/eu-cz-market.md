# Grounding pack — EU + Czechia C&I energy market

All figures are **illustrative orders of magnitude** for teaching. Tell learners to verify live
figures (OTE, ENTSO-E, ERÚ, EU Funding & Tenders Portal) before quoting them to a client.

## Market mechanics
- **Marginal pricing**: EU wholesale power is priced at the cost of the last unit dispatched —
  usually a **gas plant**. So gas sets the power price even when most generation is cheap renewables.
- **2022 shock**: gas spike pushed wholesale power to €300–400+/MWh at peak; reverted to roughly
  €60–110/MWh range since, but structurally above the pre-2021 €40–50 norm. Volatility is the point.
- **Spot vs PPA**: spot (day-ahead on **OTE** in CZ, coupled to the EU **single day-ahead market**)
  is volatile; a **PPA** (Power Purchase Agreement) fixes a price for 5–15 yrs.
- **Czech retail/industrial all-in tariff**: order of magnitude **€100–180/MWh** depending on
  voltage level, network charges, and the regulated **ERÚ** components. Network + levies are a big
  slice on top of the energy commodity.

## Products & numbers (illustrative)
- **Solar PV capex (utility/C&I, CZ/EU)**: ~€500–750/kWp installed. Rooftop C&I a bit higher.
- **Specific yield (CZ)**: ~950–1,100 kWh/kWp/yr (lower than Iberia's ~1,500+).
- **Solar LCOE (CZ)**: ~€50–70/MWh → typically **below** the industrial grid tariff = a real saving.
- **BESS capex**: ~€250–450/kWh (falling). Revenue = **stacked**: energy arbitrage + capacity +
  ancillary services (e.g. **FCR** frequency containment). Round-trip efficiency ~85–90%.
- **Round-trip efficiency** for Li-ion BESS: ~86%.

## PPA structures
- **On-site / behind-the-meter physical**: panels on the client's roof/land; they buy the kWh
  directly, avoiding network charges. Biggest saving, smallest scale.
- **Sleeved PPA**: a utility "sleeves" off-site generation to the client through the grid.
- **Virtual / financial PPA (a CfD — Contract for Difference)**: purely financial hedge; the
  client keeps buying from the grid but settles the difference vs a strike price. Common for
  corporates wanting a fixed price + Guarantees of Origin without physical delivery.
- **EaaS (Energy-as-a-Service)**: no capex for the client; they pay €/kWh or a service fee, the
  developer owns the asset. The "no-capex" pitch.

## Regulation / demand drivers (real)
- **RED III** (Renewable Energy Directive, 2023): raises the EU 2030 renewables target to ~42.5%
  (aim 45%); faster permitting; renewables "overriding public interest".
- **CBAM** (Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism): carbon cost on imports → EU industry needs clean
  power to stay competitive. Definitive regime phasing in to 2026+.
- **EU ETS**: industrial CO₂ has a price (often €60–90/t range); cutting Scope 2 emissions has
  direct value.
- **Scope 2 emissions**: emissions from purchased electricity — a board-level reporting liability
  under CSRD. PPAs + Guarantees of Origin cut them.
- **Guarantees of Origin (GOs)**: tradable certificates proving 1 MWh came from renewables.

## Czechia specifics (real institutions)
- **OTE, a.s.** — Czech electricity & gas market operator (spot market, GOs registry).
- **ERÚ** (Energetický regulační úřad) — the energy regulator; sets regulated network tariffs.
- **CEPS** — the transmission system operator (TSO).
- **MPO** (Ministerstvo průmyslu a obchodu) — Ministry of Industry & Trade; energy policy + funds.
- **SFŽP ČR** (Státní fond životního prostředí) — State Environmental Fund; **operates the
  Modernisation Fund** programmes in CZ.

## Exit / project finance
- **Development premium**: value created by taking a project from idea → operating asset.
- **Yield compression**: funds buy de-risked operating assets at a **lower discount rate** (~6–8%)
  than developers required to build (~10–13%). Same cash, lower rate → higher price = the exit gain.
- **DSCR** (Debt Service Coverage Ratio): cashflow ÷ debt service; lenders want ~1.2–1.4x+.
- **Buyers**: infrastructure funds, pension funds, listed YieldCos, utilities buying pipeline.
