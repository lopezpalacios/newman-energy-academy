Reference

Glossary

Every key term in the course, in plain English. If a word in a lesson is unfamiliar, it is defined here.

Market & products

PPA

Power Purchase Agreement — a contract to buy electricity at a fixed (or formula) price for a set term, usually 5–15 years. Replaces volatile grid pricing with certainty.

Spot market

The short-term wholesale market where power is traded day-ahead. In Czechia it is run by OTE and coupled to the EU single day-ahead market. Volatile by nature.

Marginal pricing

Wholesale power is priced at the cost of the last (most expensive) plant needed — usually gas. So gas often sets the price even when most supply is cheap renewables.

LCOE

Levelised Cost of Energy — the all-in cost per unit (€/MWh) of power from an asset over its life, including capex and operating cost. The number you compare against the grid tariff.

Capex / Opex

Capital expenditure (the upfront build cost, e.g. €/kWp) and operating expenditure (annual running cost — maintenance, lease, insurance).

Specific yield

How much a solar plant generates per unit of capacity, in kWh per kWp per year. Czechia ≈ 950–1,100; sunnier Iberia ≈ 1,500+.

BESS

Battery Energy Storage System. Stores power and sells it later. Rated in MW (power) and MWh (energy); the ratio is its duration.

Revenue stacking

Earning a battery several revenues at once: energy arbitrage, capacity payments, and ancillary services (e.g. FCR frequency response).

Round-trip efficiency

The share of energy a battery returns after storing it — Li-ion ≈ 86%. The rest is lost in charge/discharge.

Sleeved PPA

A utility delivers off-site generation to a customer through the grid, "sleeving" it into their supply.

Virtual PPA (CfD)

A purely financial PPA — a Contract for Difference. The customer keeps buying from the grid but settles the gap versus a strike price. No physical delivery.

EaaS

Energy-as-a-Service — the developer owns the asset and the customer pays per kWh or a service fee. The "no-capex" offer.

Regulation & demand

RED III

The EU Renewable Energy Directive (2023). Raises the 2030 renewables target to ~42.5% and speeds up permitting.

CBAM

Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism — a carbon cost on imports into the EU, pushing industry toward clean power to stay competitive.

EU ETS

The Emissions Trading System — Europe's carbon market, putting a price on industrial CO₂ emissions.

Scope 2

Emissions from the electricity a company buys. A reporting liability under CSRD that renewable PPAs directly reduce.

Guarantees of Origin

Tradable certificates proving a MWh came from renewables — how green claims are substantiated.

Grid parity

The point where new solar/storage costs less than buying from the grid — so going green also lowers the bill.

Finance & exit

NPV

Net Present Value — the sum of all future cash flows discounted to today. The rational price for an asset.

DCF

Discounted Cash Flow — the method of valuing an asset by discounting its future cash to present value.

Discount rate

The return a buyer requires, used to discount future cash. Lower rate → higher value on the same cash flows.

IRR

Internal Rate of Return — the annualised return a project earns. Compared against a "hurdle rate" to decide whether to build.

DSCR

Debt Service Coverage Ratio — cash flow divided by debt payments. Lenders want roughly 1.2–1.4× or more.

Yield compression

When a buyer values an asset at a lower required yield than the developer did — turning the same cash flows into a higher price. The exit gain.

Development premium

The value created by taking a project from idea to operating asset, through each de-risking gate.

COD

Commercial Operation Date — when a plant is finished, energised and selling power. The moment risk drops sharply.

TSO / DSO

Transmission / Distribution System Operator — who runs the grid you connect to. In Czechia the TSO is CEPS.

EU funding

Modernisation Fund

An EU fund (financed by carbon allowances) for clean energy in 10 lower-income member states including Czechia. In CZ it is operated by SFŽP ČR.

Innovation Fund

An EU fund for large-scale and first-of-a-kind clean-tech projects, awarded through competitive EU-wide calls.

RRF

Recovery and Resilience Facility — EU recovery money spent via each country's National Recovery Plan (CZ: Národní plán obnovy).

InvestEU / EIB

An EU guarantee programme delivered with the European Investment Bank — cheap debt and guarantees rather than grants.

OP TAK

Operační program Technologie a aplikace pro konkurenceschopnost — Czech ERDF programme (run by MPO/API) with energy calls for business.

Co-financing

A grant usually funds only part of eligible capex; the applicant brings the rest. Subsidy is capped by EU state-aid rules.
