Abbreviations, decoded

Every acronym in commercial energy in one place. Search it, or filter by topic. Each one in a single plain-English line.

PPAPower Purchase Agreement

A contract to buy electricity at a fixed or formula price for 5–15 years, replacing volatile grid pricing.

Market
MWhMegawatt-hour

The unit electricity is sold in — one MWh = 1,000 kWh. Wholesale prices are quoted in €/MWh.

Market
kWhKilowatt-hour

The retail unit of energy — what a household or small load is billed on.

Market
MWMegawatt

A unit of power (capacity) — how big a plant is, not how much it produces.

Market
kWpKilowatt-peak

A solar array's rated peak output. Capex is often quoted per kWp.

Market
OTEOperátor trhu s elektřinou

The Czech electricity & gas market operator — runs the spot market and the Guarantees-of-Origin registry.

Market
TSOTransmission System Operator

Runs the high-voltage grid. In Czechia that is ČEPS.

Market
DSODistribution System Operator

Runs the local lower-voltage network most C&I sites connect to.

Market
SDACSingle Day-Ahead Coupling

The mechanism that links EU national day-ahead power markets into one coupled market.

Market
GOGuarantee of Origin

A tradable certificate proving one MWh came from renewables — how green claims are backed.

Market
PVPhotovoltaic

Solar panels that convert sunlight directly into electricity.

Products
BESSBattery Energy Storage System

A grid battery — stores power and sells it later. Rated in MW (power) and MWh (energy).

Products
EaaSEnergy-as-a-Service

The developer owns the asset; the customer pays per kWh or a service fee. The no-capex offer.

Products
FCRFrequency Containment Reserve

A fast grid-balancing service batteries get paid to provide — part of the revenue stack.

Products
O&MOperations & Maintenance

The ongoing cost of running an asset — cleaning, monitoring, repairs, insurance.

Products
RTERound-Trip Efficiency

The share of energy a battery returns after storing it — Li-ion ≈ 86%.

Products
CfDContract for Difference

A financial PPA — the customer settles the gap vs a strike price, with no physical delivery.

Products
LCOELevelised Cost of Energy

The all-in cost per MWh of power from an asset over its life — the number you beat the grid with.

Finance
NPVNet Present Value

Future cash flows discounted to today and summed — the rational price for an asset.

Finance
DCFDiscounted Cash Flow

Valuing an asset by discounting its future cash to present value.

Finance
IRRInternal Rate of Return

The annualised return a project earns — compared against a hurdle rate to decide whether to build.

Finance
DSCRDebt Service Coverage Ratio

Cash flow ÷ debt payments. Lenders want roughly 1.2–1.4× or more.

Finance
CAPEXCapital Expenditure

The upfront build cost of an asset, often quoted per kWp or per kWh.

Finance
OPEXOperating Expenditure

The annual running cost — maintenance, lease, insurance, monitoring.

Finance
MOICMultiple on Invested Capital

Money returned ÷ money invested — e.g. selling for 1.36× what you spent.

Finance
WACCWeighted Average Cost of Capital

The blended cost of a buyer's debt and equity — drives the discount rate they value assets at.

Finance
RED IIIRenewable Energy Directive (2023)

EU law raising the 2030 renewables target to ~42.5% and speeding up permitting.

Regulation
CBAMCarbon Border Adjustment Mechanism

A carbon cost on imports into the EU, pushing industry toward clean power.

Regulation
ETSEmissions Trading System

Europe's carbon market — puts a price on industrial CO₂ emissions.

Regulation
CSRDCorporate Sustainability Reporting Directive

EU rules requiring companies to report emissions — making Scope 2 a board-level liability.

Regulation
EIAEnvironmental Impact Assessment

A permitting study required for larger projects before they can be built.

Regulation
GBERGeneral Block Exemption Regulation

EU rules that cap how much state-aid (grant) a project may receive.

Regulation
ERÚEnergetický regulační úřad

The Czech energy regulator — sets the regulated network tariff components.

Czechia
ČEPSCzech transmission operator

Czechia's high-voltage TSO — runs the backbone grid you interconnect to.

Czechia
MPOMinisterstvo průmyslu a obchodu

Czech Ministry of Industry & Trade — energy policy and the OP TAK programme.

Czechia
SFŽPStátní fond životního prostředí ČR

State Environmental Fund — operates the Modernisation Fund and RES+ in Czechia.

Czechia
APIAgentura pro podnikání a inovace

The agency administering OP TAK business calls in Czechia.

Czechia
NRBNárodní rozvojová banka

The Czech national development bank — co-finances and guarantees projects.

Czechia
RRFRecovery and Resilience Facility

EU recovery money spent via each country's National Recovery Plan (CZ: Národní plán obnovy).

EU funding
EIBEuropean Investment Bank

The EU's bank — project loans, green finance and advisory, often the senior lender.

EU funding
RES+Renewable Energy Sources programme

A Czech Modernisation Fund programme (run by SFŽP) funding new solar and other renewables.

EU funding
OP TAKOperační program Technologie a aplikace pro konkurenceschopnost

Czech ERDF programme (2021–27) with energy-saving and renewables calls for business.

EU funding
CEFConnecting Europe Facility

EU funding for cross-border infrastructure, including energy (CEF-Energy).

EU funding
LIFELIFE Programme

EU grants for environment and clean-energy transition — smaller, capacity-building.

EU funding
CODCommercial Operation Date

When a plant is finished, energised and selling power — risk drops sharply here.

Project
RTBReady to Build

The stage where permits, grid and offtake are secured and construction can start.

Project
FCFinancial Close

When all financing is signed and funds can be drawn to build.

Project
EPCEngineering, Procurement & Construction

The contractor that designs and builds the plant under a fixed-price contract.

Project
SPASale and Purchase Agreement

The contract that transfers ownership of an asset to a buyer at exit.

Project
IMInformation Memorandum

The document marketing an asset to potential buyers in a sale process.

Project
NBONon-Binding Offer

An early indicative bid from a buyer before full due diligence.

Project

Germany's renewables law — supports clean power via a market premium and competitive auctions; targets 80% renewable electricity by 2030.

DACH
KfWKreditanstalt für Wiederaufbau

Germany's state development bank — a source of cheap project finance for clean energy.

DACH

German rule giving controllable loads (heat pumps, EV chargers, home storage) reduced grid fees in exchange for being dimmable.

Austria's energy regulator.

Austria's transmission system operator (TSO).

DACH

ElComElektrizitätskommission

Switzerland's electricity regulator.

Switzerland's transmission system operator (TSO).
